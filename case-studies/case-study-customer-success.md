# Case Study — Customer Success & Adoption Program

*Sanitized and synthetic. Illustrative of real work; no client data included.*

## Problem

A team had rolled out a new tool/workflow but adoption was stalling. Usage was shallow, a few skeptical staff were vocal, and leadership couldn't tell whether the investment was paying off. Without a clear success plan or health signals, the risk of quiet abandonment (and churn) was rising.

## Approach

- **Ran structured discovery** to surface the real outcome leadership cared about and the blockers underneath the resistance.
- **Built a 30/60/90-day success plan** tied to that outcome, with baseline metrics captured before changes.
- **Stood up a health-score model** across adoption, outcomes, engagement, relationship, and sentiment — with a rule that a single critical signal (e.g., a departing champion) overrides a good average.
- **Addressed adoption as change management:** named champions, a clear approval rule, weekly check-ins, and public wins.
- **Structured QBRs and stakeholder mapping** so value was visible to everyone who needed to see it, not just the day-to-day user.

## Result (qualitative)

- Adoption deepened from surface use to the full workflow, including the approval step
- Early risk signals became visible before they turned into churn
- Leadership could see value in metrics against baseline, not anecdotes
- The account moved from single-threaded and fragile to multi-stakeholder and durable

## What it demonstrates

Onboarding and success planning, health-score modeling, adoption-risk management, QBR and stakeholder discipline, and executive/KPI reporting.
