# Discovery Question Bank — Healthcare Customer Success

A structured bank of discovery questions for onboarding and expanding healthcare and home-care accounts. Good discovery is the difference between a tool that gets adopted and one that gets shelved. Ask, then listen.

## 1. Business & goals

- What does success with this look like 90 days from now? A year from now?
- What's the one outcome that, if we nail it, makes this worth it?
- How will your leadership judge whether this worked?
- What's changed recently that made solving this a priority now?

## 2. Current state & pain

- Walk me through how this workflow happens today, step by step.
- Where does it break down or eat the most time?
- What have you already tried, and why didn't it stick?
- Who feels this pain most day to day?

## 3. Stakeholders & decision-making

- Besides you, who needs to see value for this to be considered a win?
- Who could quietly block adoption if they're not on board?
- Who's the clinical / compliance voice we need aligned early?
- How do decisions like this usually get made here?

## 4. Workflow & integration

- What tools does your team already live in?
- Where does data come from and where does it need to go?
- What can't change because of compliance or existing process?
- What's the realistic appetite for new steps in someone's day?

## 5. Adoption & risk

- Who are your most influential frontline staff — champions or skeptics?
- What's your team's comfort level with new technology, honestly?
- What would make staff distrust or abandon this?
- How much training time can you realistically protect?

## 6. Compliance & privacy (healthcare-specific)

- What data is in scope, and what absolutely cannot leave your systems?
- Who owns the compliance sign-off?
- What's your rule for anything patient-facing?

## 7. Expansion & advocacy (post-value)

- Now that this works here, where else does the same problem exist?
- Who else on your team should see this?
- Would you be comfortable being a reference for peers?

## How to use this

Don't fire all of these — pick the 6–8 that fit the moment. The goal is to surface the real outcome, the real blockers, and the compliance line before you design the plan.
