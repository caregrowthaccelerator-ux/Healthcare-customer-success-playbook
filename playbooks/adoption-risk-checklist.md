# Adoption-Risk Checklist

## User behavior

- [ ] Priority users have logged in
- [ ] Users complete the core workflow
- [ ] Usage is distributed beyond one champion
- [ ] Repeat usage is increasing
- [ ] Required training is complete
- [ ] Support volume is stable or declining

## Stakeholders

- [ ] Executive sponsor remains engaged
- [ ] Day-to-day owner has sufficient authority
- [ ] Clinical, operational, technical, and compliance stakeholders are aligned
- [ ] No critical stakeholder has changed roles without a replacement
- [ ] Decision-makers understand delivered value

## Outcomes

- [ ] Success metrics have owners and data sources
- [ ] The customer can describe measurable progress
- [ ] The workflow addresses a current priority
- [ ] New risks or competing initiatives are documented
- [ ] Renewal value is being demonstrated before renewal discussions

## Risk signals

| Signal | Severity | Suggested response |
|---|---:|---|
| Sponsor disengagement | High | Reconfirm executive outcomes and schedule sponsor alignment |
| Champion-only usage | High | Expand role-based training and identify secondary champions |
| No measurable outcome | High | Rebuild the success plan around a verifiable business metric |
| Training completed, usage low | Medium | Observe workflow friction and deliver use-case coaching |
| Rising support volume | Medium | Identify root causes and prioritize recurring blockers |
| Delayed integration | High | Create an executive dependency plan with owner and date |
| Stakeholder turnover | Medium | Re-map influence and conduct a targeted re-onboarding |
